Hula Girl

When you are craving a tropical escape in a glass, look no further than this vibrant dirty soda recipe, brought to you directly by the KazziSoda team broadcasting from the soda capital of the world: Provo, Utah. This specific mix is absolutely one of my personal favorites! There is just something incredibly indulgent about transforming a crisp lemon-lime soda into an aesthetic, creamy luau in a cup. Today, we are putting the spotlight on the "Hula Girl," a drink that guarantees sunshine in every single sip.

What is a Hula Girl Dirty Soda?

A Hula Girl Dirty Soda is a bright, island-inspired beverage that blends crisp, citrusy Sprite with rich pineapple and coconut syrups. It is topped off with a velvety sweet cream float, creating a delightfully sweet and refreshing tropical treat.

The Vibe Check

Every drink at KazziSoda tells a story, and the Hula Girl is screaming Luau Time. Picture yourself relaxing under palm trees with a fresh lei, but captured perfectly in a frosted glass. It's highly sweet, delightfully creamy, and has just a tiny hint of citrus tartness to keep things balanced.

Sweetness 5/5
Tartness 2/5

Your Ingredients

To build this stunning, Instagram-worthy beverage, gather these essentials for your home soda bar:

  • Sprite (or your preferred lemon-lime sparkling soda)
  • Premium Pineapple Syrup
  • Premium Coconut Syrup
  • Sweet Cream (half and half or a rich coconut creamer)
  • Ice (pebble ice is an absolute must for that classic soda shop experience!)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Crafting this recipe is all about the aesthetic mix. Follow these simple steps for the perfect pour:

  1. Prep the glass: Pack your favorite aesthetic glass all the way to the brim with pebble ice.
  2. Mix the syrups: Pour both the pineapple syrup and the coconut syrup directly over the ice, allowing them to mingle at the bottom of the glass.
  3. Pour the soda: Slowly top with your Sprite, watching the bright carbonation lift and mix those tropical syrup flavors.
  4. The Mixologist's Secret: Personal Tip: I make this recipe constantly since it is one of my absolute personal favorites! But when I want to switch things up, I completely swap the pineapple out for peach syrup. It gives the drink a sweet "Georgia" twist while maintaining that lush coconut-cream base. You have to try both versions!
  5. Float the cream: Gently cascade your sweet cream over the top. Watch those gorgeous, cloudy ribbons sink into the clear soda before giving it all a satisfying stir.
