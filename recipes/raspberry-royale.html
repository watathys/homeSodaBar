Raspberry Royale

If you are looking for an elevated dirty soda recipe, the KazziSoda team is broadcasting from the undisputed soda capital of the world: Provo, Utah, to bring you our most sophisticated creation yet. The Raspberry Royale is designed for those moments when a standard fountain drink won't cut it—this is high-end mixology in a pebble-ice glass.

What is a Raspberry Royale Dirty Soda?

The Raspberry Royale is a sophisticated mocktail that pairs the crisp, sharp carbonation of Diet Coke with a balanced blend of tart raspberry and smooth vanilla. Topped with a heavy pour of sweet cream, it creates a rich, marbled aesthetic that tastes like a high-end dessert.

The Flavor Profile

This isn't just a quick sugar fix; it's a curated experience. The Raspberry Royale hits the sweet spot between a refreshing fruit drink and a decadent cream soda.

Vibe Fancy Dinner
Sweetness 3/5
Tartness 3/5
Creaminess 4/5

Ingredients

  • Base: 12 oz Diet Coke (chilled)
  • Fruit: 1.5 pumps Raspberry Syrup
  • Sweetener: 1.5 pumps Vanilla Syrup
  • The "Dirty" Factor: 2 tbsp Heavy Sweet Cream
  • Ice: Fresh pebble ice (the "Good Ice")

How to Make a Raspberry Royale

  1. Prep the Glass: Fill a 16 oz glass to the brim with pebble ice. Listen for that distinct, hollow crunch—the ice is the foundation of the texture.
  2. Layer the Flavors: Pump your raspberry and vanilla syrups directly over the ice. The syrups should pool slightly at the bottom, creating a concentrated flavor base.
  3. The Pour: Slow-pour your Diet Coke over the ice. Watch for the heavy carbonation to react with the syrup; you want a thick, fizzy head to form but not overflow.
  4. The "Royale" Finish: Pour the sweet cream directly into the center of the glass.
  5. Personal Visual Tip: Do not stir immediately. Watch the cream sink through the dark soda in white, ghostly plumes. This "marbling" effect is the hallmark of a professional dirty soda and looks stunning under dining room lights.
  6. Garnish (Optional): Drop a single fresh raspberry on top to lean into that "Fancy Dinner" aesthetic.

Mix It Up

If you want to lean further into the "Royale" theme but prefer something a bit lighter, swap the Raspberry Syrup for Peach Syrup. The peach creates a softer, sun-drenched flavor profile that feels more like a garden party than a black-tie gala.
