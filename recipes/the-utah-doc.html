The Utah Doc

If you are searching for the definitive dirty soda recipe, you have officially reached the source: the KazziSoda team is broadcasting live from the soda capital of the world, Provo, Utah.

In this corner of the mountain west, soda isn’t just a drink—it’s a lifestyle. We don’t do bland, and we certainly don't do "basic." We’re here to show you how to master the drink that put the 801 area code on the map.

What is The Utah Doc Dirty Soda?

The Utah Doc is the foundational "Utah Original" mocktail, featuring a base of cold Dr Pepper enhanced with tropical coconut and fresh citrus. It is a high-contrast treat that perfectly balances the deep, spicy notes of the soda with a bright, tart finish.

The Vibe: The Utah Original

This is the drink that launched a thousand soda shops. It’s reliable, refreshing, and carries an unmistakable aesthetic of dark carbonation clashing with neon-bright lime.

Sweetness 3/5
Tartness 3/5
Creaminess 1/5

Ingredients

  • Dr Pepper: Your favorite spicy cherry-cola base.
  • Coconut Syrup: A high-quality, translucent syrup (we recommend Torani or Monin).
  • Fresh Lime: Skip the bottled juice; you need the real thing.
  • Pebble Ice: Crucial for the authentic "crunch" and cold retention.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prep the Vessel: Fill a 24oz glass to the brim with pebble ice. The smaller surface area of the ice ensures your drink stays frigid without diluting the carbonation too quickly.
  2. The Citrus Hit: Squeeze half of a fresh lime directly over the ice. Don’t be afraid to drop the spent lime wedge right into the glass for that extra punch of essential oils.
  3. The Sweetener: Pump 1–2 ounces of coconut syrup over the ice and lime.
  4. The Pour: Slowly pour your Dr Pepper over the mixture.
  5. Personal Visual Tip: To achieve that perfect "Instagrammable" swirl, pour the soda slowly down the side of the glass. You’ll watch the clear coconut syrup rise and marble through the dark caramel bubbles of the Dr Pepper.
  6. The Finish: Give it one gentle stir. You want to incorporate the flavors without killing the "fizz" factor.

Mix It Up: The Malibu Style

If you want to lean into the "Creamy" factor, add a half-ounce splash of Vanilla Syrup. This softens the bite of the lime and gives the drink a smoother, "Malibu" style finish that tastes like a vacation in a cup.
