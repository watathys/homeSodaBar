Pineapple Express

Finding the perfect dirty soda recipe is easy when you are learning from the KazziSoda team, broadcasting directly from the soda capital of the world: Provo, Utah. While others are just discovering the magic of mixed carbonation, we’ve been refining the ratios here in the 801 for years.

If you’re looking for a drink that feels like a tropical vacation served in a foam cup, the Pineapple Express is your new go-to. It’s thick, indulgent, and unapologetically sweet.

What is a Pineapple Express Dirty Soda?

The Pineapple Express is a tropical "Tiki Cola" mocktail that combines the caramel notes of Coca-Cola with pineapple and coconut syrups. This specific recipe is defined by a heavy float of half-and-half, creating a rich, creamy texture that mimics a frozen island cocktail.

The Vibe: Tiki Cola

This isn’t a light refreshment; it’s a dessert in a glass. The Pineapple Express leans heavily into the "creamy" side of the dirty soda spectrum, offering a lush mouthfeel that cuts through the sharp bubbles of the cola.

Sweetness 4/5
Tartness 2/5
Creaminess 4/5

Ingredients

  • Coca-Cola: The classic red-label base provides the necessary bite.
  • Pineapple Syrup: A bright, fruity syrup to drive the tropical profile.
  • Coconut Syrup: The essential "dirty" component for that island flair.
  • Half & Half: Use full-fat dairy for the most luxurious texture.
  • Pebble Ice: Essential for creating the cold surface area needed for the cream float.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. The Foundation: Pack your glass to the brim with pebble ice. The crunch of the ice is half the experience, and it provides the structure needed to layer your ingredients.
  2. The Tropical Base: Pump one ounce of pineapple syrup and one ounce of coconut syrup directly over the ice.
  3. The Sparkle: Pour cold Coca-Cola over the syrups. Leave about an inch of space at the top of the glass for the finale.
  4. The Signature Float: Slowly pour the half-and-half over the center of the ice.
  5. Personal Visual Tip: Watch closely as the white cream hits the dark soda. It will create a "lava lamp" effect, cascading down the sides of the ice cubes in heavy, marbleized ribbons before eventually settling into a beautiful tan hue.
  6. The Sip: Do not stir this drink vigorously. A single, gentle swirl with a straw keeps the layers distinct so you get a hit of pure cream followed by tropical cola in every sip.

Mix It Up: The Skinny Tiki

If you want the tropical flavor without the heavy dairy, swap the classic Coke for Diet Coke and replace the half-and-half with a fresh lime squeeze. This "Skinny Tiki" version brings the tartness up to a 4 and keeps the vibe light and zesty.
