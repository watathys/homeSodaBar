Amalfi Splash

Finding the perfect dirty soda recipe is easy when the KazziSoda team is broadcasting directly from the soda capital of the world: Provo, Utah. Here in the land of drive-thru soda shops and 44-ounce cups, we know that a true "dirty" drink isn't just a beverage—it's a lifestyle. While many local favorites rely heavily on coconut cream, our latest creation, Amalfi Splash, proves that you can get that signature "dirty" kick with a sharp, Mediterranean citrus twist.

What is an Amalfi Splash Dirty Soda?

Amalfi Splash is a tart, sparkling-water-based dirty soda designed for those who crave a sophisticated, citrus-heavy profile. By layering sweet strawberry syrup with the acidic bite of fresh lemon and lime, it delivers a vibrant, Mediterranean-inspired treat that skips the cream but keeps the bold flavor intensity.

The Vibe: Mediterranean Escape

If you're dreaming of a cliffside terrace in Positano but you're currently in your kitchen, this is the drink for you. It's sharp, refreshing, and unapologetically bold.

Sweetness 1/5
Tartness 3/5
Creaminess 1/5

What You'll Need

  • Pebble Ice: Crucial for the authentic Provo texture.
  • Sparkling Water: Use a high-carbonation seltzer for maximum fizz.
  • Strawberry Syrup: 2-3 pumps of your favorite premium brand.
  • Fresh Lemon: Half a lemon, sliced into wedges.
  • Fresh Lime: Half a lime, sliced into wedges.

Mixing the Amalfi Splash

  1. Prep the Glass: Fill a tall glass to the very brim with pebble ice. Listen for that distinct, hollow crunch—that's how you know the ice is right.
  2. The Strawberry Base: Pour your strawberry syrup directly over the ice. Watch as the ruby-red syrup cascades through the cubes, pooling at the bottom in a thick, sweet layer.
  3. The Citrus Punch: Squeeze two fresh lemon wedges and two lime wedges directly into the glass. Drop the spent wedges into the ice for extra zest.
  4. Personal Visual Tip: For a professional, high-aesthetic look, use a bar spoon to press a few thin citrus wheels flat against the inside of the glass before adding your ice. It creates a "botanical" look that makes the drink pop on camera.
  5. The Sparkle: Top the glass with chilled sparkling water. You'll see the aggressive carbonation hit the syrup, causing a "bloom" of red color that swirls upward through the ice.
  6. The Final Stir: Give it one quick, gentle stir. You want a gradient effect—deep red at the bottom fading into a pale, sunset pink at the top.

Mix It Up: The Strawberry Colada Spritz

If you want to lean into the "dirty" tradition, add one pump of coconut syrup. This transforms the drink into a Strawberry Colada Spritz, adding a smooth, tropical finish that plays perfectly against the tart lime.
